# Requirement Clarification

## Goal

Turn a loose idea into a concrete product direction before design work starts.

## Clarify

Identify or infer:

- Product category: app, SaaS, tool, marketplace, content product, internal system, etc.
- Target user: role, skill level, situation, motivation, and frequency of use.
- Core problem: what pain, job, or workflow the product improves.
- Product promise: the concise outcome the product offers.
- Business or creator goal: retention, paid conversion, operational efficiency, lead generation, habit formation, etc.
- Platform: iOS SwiftUI, web Element UI, both, or unknown.
- Constraints: brand, data source, region, privacy, integrations, monetization, timeline.

## Ask Fewer, Better Questions

Ask questions only when the answer changes the product architecture or MVP:

- Who is the primary user?
- What is the most important action they repeat?
- What must be tracked, created, reviewed, or managed?
- What platform should the first version target?
- Is this consumer-facing, coach/operator-facing, or both?

If the user is exploring, make assumptions and continue. Mark assumptions explicitly.

## MVP Boundary

Define:

- Must-have features for the first usable loop.
- Nice-to-have features to postpone.
- Non-goals that would distract the first version.
- Risky assumptions to validate.

Avoid turning the MVP into a feature catalog.
